Some of the Things I Learned in 2017
folder [Coaching]   label Reflection   Learning   Lookback  

One of the most important habits I have been building over the past couple years has been to be more deliberate in my reflection. So I wanted to close out this year with an inventory of what I’ve learned, both to solidify it and to figure out where I might spend my efforts next year. To figure out how to structure such a list, I turned to my daily practice modeled on the one suggested by James Altucher, but with a few additions. These are the areas of my life I have defined as important enough that I need to learn and grow in them every day, so it makes sense to reflect on them here at year end. This list is certainly not exhaustive, but hopefully captures some of the most important lessons.

General

  • James Altucher’s “Daily Practice”
  • Bullet Journaling
  • Don’t pursue “success” that rewards you with less of what you like and more of what you don’t
  • Specific storytelling skills
  • The Life Changing Power of Checklists
  • The Extreme Utility of Simple Rules
  • Buffet’s “Two Lists”
  • The Ten Year Plan for a Remarkable Life 
  • Tracking “Blocks” of Focus Time for Productivity

Mental

  • Journaling to figure things out
  • The importance of “liquid networks” to creativity and innovation
  • The “moist robot hypothesis”
  • Systems, not goals
  • How to use “virtual mentors” 
  • Gretchen Rubin’s Four Motivational Tendencies
  • Linking insights from military history and business operating model work
  • Breadth first versus depth first searching
  • What a “folksonomy” is
  • Challenge Mapping
  • Basic Copywriting skills
  • The application of evolutionary psychology to military history 
  • The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing
  • How England’s sea power and conflict with France shaped the modern world
  • Predictability and Computational Capacity as a Definition of Free Will
  • About affordances and signifiers in design

Emotional

  • Journaling as a mood stabilizer
  • Shawn Achor’s Five Happiness Habits
  • Happiness = Reality - Expectations
  • A comprehensive strategy for dealing with stress
  • The Color Code Hartman Personality Profile
  • How it feels to apply Stoic principles to extremely bad news

Reading

  • That for me, reading is must, not a nice to have
  • The importance of a daily reading habit
  • How to effectively take and organize notes
  • The joys of an “inverse book club”

Physical

  • The health consequences of sitting
  • How to properly align my spine
  • How to do some yoga
  • The Happy Body 

Spiritual

  • The deep relaxation of a flotation tank
  • A deeper knowledge of Taoism
  • An expanded view of meditation
  • A functioning definition of “spiritual” outside of a religious context
  • The Consolations of Philosophy
  • How to Track My Virtues
  • About Heart Rate Variability Training

Conclusion

I hope that you have a wonderful new year full of hope and opportunity! What have you learned this year, and what might you learn in the next?
